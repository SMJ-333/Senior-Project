About Our Museum

Discover the rich history, mission, and people behind the ATHAR Museum, a cultural landmark dedicated to preserving and sharing our collective ATHAR.

Our History

1892

Foundation

The ATHAR Museum was established through a generous donation from industrialist Johnathan ATHAR, who envisioned a space where history could be preserved and shared with future generations.

1925

First Expansion

The museum underwent its first major expansion, adding the West Wing to accommodate growing collections of ancient artifacts from Egyptian and Mediterranean excavations.

1954

Post-War Renaissance

After temporary closure during the war years, the museum reopened with renewed focus on education and community outreach programs.

1987

Modernization Project

A comprehensive modernization updated facilities while preserving the historic architecture, including climate-controlled galleries and improved accessibility.

2015

Digital Initiative

Launched our digital archives, making over 25,000 artifacts accessible online to researchers and enthusiasts worldwide.

2023

Sustainability Commitment

Became the first museum in the region to achieve carbon neutrality through renewable energy and sustainable practices.

Common Questions

How do I book a group visit?

Group visits (10+ people) can be booked by calling our Group Sales department at (555) 123-GROUP or by emailing groups@ATHARmuseum.org. We recommend booking at least two weeks in advance.

Do you offer guided tours?

Yes, we offer guided tours daily at 11:00 AM and 2:00 PM. Private tours can be arranged for groups of 6 or more with advance booking. Audio guides are also available for rent at the entrance.

Can I host an event at the museum?

Absolutely! Our museum spaces are available for private events, corporate functions, and weddings. Please contact our events team at events@ATHARmuseum.org for more information and availability.

How can I support the museum?

There are several ways to support us: become a member, make a donation, volunteer your time, or sponsor an exhibition. Visit our Support section on the website or contact development@ATHARmuseum.org for more details.
